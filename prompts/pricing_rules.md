# Pricing Rules

## Goal
Price for ~1 month sell-through on Vinted UK. Aim for the price buyers actually pay —
not too cheap (leaves money on table), not too high (sits unsold).

## Vinted Fees
Vinted charges ~5% + £0.70 per sale. Factor this into your target net.

## Pricing Method — Market-First

**Step 1 — Find the right market band** using the reference table below.
Pick the row that matches item type and brand tier (luxury vs high street).

**Step 2 — Position within the band:**
- Standard brand / average condition: lower 40% of the range
- Good brand or good condition: midpoint
- Luxury/designer brand (see list below) in very good condition: upper 25–30% of the range
- Exceptional piece (rare colourway, pristine, tags attached): at or near ceiling

**Step 3 — Sanity check vs buy price:**
The list price must never be less than 3× the buy price (absolute minimum floor).
This is a floor only — do not use the buy price as the main pricing driver.

## Luxury Brand List
These brands sit at the upper end of the ceiling range. Do not discount unless unsold at 30 days:
- Ermenegildo Zegna, Canali, Corneliani, Brioni, Kiton
- Barbour, Belstaff, Grenfell, Gloverall
- Margaret Howell, Brora, John Smedley

## Seasonal Adjustment
- Out-of-season: price 15% below your target to move faster.
- Peak season (Oct–Nov for coats, Apr–May for lightweight): price at ceiling.

## Example Calculations
- Zegna blazer (luxury), very good condition → range £55–£150, upper band → **list at £125**
- Barbour wax jacket C42, very good → range £60–£140, good condition → **list at £95**
- Hugo Boss blazer, good condition → range £55–£150, mid brand → **list at £65**
- Standard M&S blazer → range £20–£55, mid → **list at £32**
- Levi's 501 jeans → range £16–£45, mid → **list at £28**
- M&S lambswool jumper → range £18–£50, low end → **list at £22**
